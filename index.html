Mouse Sensor Guide

How to play: Use your arrow keys to move the tiles. When two stable isotopes can be fused they fuse! Radioactive isotopes cannot be fused. The number of protons and neutrons in each of the isotopes will be added to create a new isotope.

After every move, all of the radioactive isotopes on the board will decay (have their moves remaining counter) reduced by 1. If this counter reaches 0, the isotope decays into a new one. The background color of the moves counter indicates the type of decay:

Alpha Decay
β- Decay
β+ Decay
Neutron Emission
Proton Emission
Electron Capture
Fission (will decay randomly into one of Technetium-99, Tin-126, Selenium-79, Zirconium-93, Cesium-135, Palladium-107, or Iodine-129)
